1.0

Purpose, Scope, and Operations Architecture

Structured reference section aligned to the Natural Alternatives compliance presentation standard.

#

The OCOS Tier 3 Compliance Operations System is the operational layer of the Ontario Compliance Operating System™. It is where generated documents become a functioning workplace safety program — organized into a controlled file structure, tracked against a compliance calendar, verified through ongoing inspection and training records, and maintained through a continuous improvement cycle.

Tier 3 does not generate documents. That function belongs to Tier 2. Tier 3 receives approved documents from the Tier 2 generation workflow, files them within a controlled structure, connects them to their supporting records, and ensures they remain current, enforced, and auditable throughout their operational life.

Without Tier 3, the OCOS system is incomplete. Documents generated in Tier 2 and approved by the employer exist as isolated files — not as a system. Tier 3 is the infrastructure that makes the system operational, defensible, and capable of producing compliance evidence on demand.

Evidence is not created by the system. Evidence is the byproduct of when the system operates. An employer who runs the system produces evidence automatically. An employer who possesses the system but does not run it produces nothing.

3.0

Compliance Infrastructure — The Eight Components

Structured reference section aligned to the Natural Alternatives compliance presentation standard.

#

The Tier 3 system is organized around eight operational components. Each component serves a distinct function within the compliance program. Together, they create the infrastructure through which an employer can demonstrate that their safety program has been planned, implemented, maintained, and continuously improved.

Component 01
Organized File Structure

A controlled folder architecture that houses all approved compliance documents — policies, programs, procedures, inspection forms, training records, and corrective action logs — organized by document type, status, and version. Current documents are clearly separated from superseded versions. Every document is findable within 60 seconds.

Component 02
Training Register

A centralized record of all workplace training delivered to workers and supervisors. Captures the worker's name, date of training, topic covered, trainer identity, and acknowledgment of comprehension. The training register is the primary evidence that workers have been informed of hazards and control requirements.

Component 03
Inspection Logs

Completed inspection records demonstrating that equipment, work areas, and compliance controls have been verified through ongoing monitoring. Inspection logs connect approved procedures to field-level verification — transforming written controls into documented evidence of supervisory oversight.

Component 04
Incident Tracking

A centralized log of all workplace incidents, near-misses, and work refusals — including investigation findings, root cause determination, and corrective actions taken. Incident records are retained and linked to any document revisions they trigger.

Component 05
Corrective Action Register

A tracked log of all identified deficiencies — from inspections, incidents, worker feedback, JHSC recommendations, and regulatory findings — with assigned responsible parties, target completion dates, and closure records. Open items are visible at a glance; closed items form part of the continuous improvement record.

Component 06
Compliance Calendar

A scheduled review and action calendar that tracks all recurring compliance obligations — annual policy reviews, training renewal dates, inspection frequencies, JHSC meeting schedules, legislative monitoring checkpoints, and document regeneration cycles. The calendar converts the OCOS system from reactive to proactive.

Component 07
Document Control Register

A master index of all controlled documents within the compliance program — including document code, current version, status, authorized-by, and next review date. The register is the single source of truth for what is current, what is superseded, and what is pending review.

Component 08
JHSC / H&S Rep Records

Meeting minutes, inspection reports, recommendations, employer responses, and committee member training records. Maintains the documented evidence of the Internal Responsibility System functioning at the joint governance level — critical for demonstrating active JHSC participation during regulatory review.

4.0

Document Filing and Version Control

Structured reference section aligned to the Natural Alternatives compliance presentation standard.

#

Every approved document entering the Tier 3 system is assigned to the appropriate folder, registered in the Document Control Register, and linked to its supporting training records and inspection logs. Version control is applied immediately — the current approved version is the only version in active circulation.

Version Control Requirements

EventVersion ActionRecord Required
Initial document approvalVersion 1.0 filed as ActiveApproval sign-off, issue date, distribution list
Minor revision (content update)Version incremented (e.g., 1.1)Reason for revision, authorized by, change communication record
Major revision (scope change)New version issued (e.g., 2.0)Prior version archived, retraining delivered and recorded
Document regenerated via Tier 2New version issued as replacementPrior version archived, new approval sign-off, retraining if content changed
Document superseded / retiredMarked Superseded in registerRetained in archive — never deleted

Superseded documents are never deleted. They are moved to the archive folder with their superseded status clearly marked. In the event of a regulatory inquiry or legal proceeding, the full version history demonstrates that the system was actively maintained — not reconstructed after the fact.

5.0

Training Register and Worker Verification

Structured reference section aligned to the Natural Alternatives compliance presentation standard.

#

The training register is the employer's primary evidence that workers have been informed of workplace hazards, trained on applicable procedures, and confirmed to understand the controls that govern their work. Without a training register, a procedure is an unpublished intention. With one, it is a documented workplace control.

Training Register — Required Fields

  • Worker name and role
  • Date of training delivery
  • Training topic or document title (linked to document code)
  • Training method (in-person, toolbox talk, orientation session)
  • Trainer name and designation
  • Worker acknowledgment signature or recorded confirmation
  • Renewal date (where applicable — e.g., annual WHMIS, equipment re-certification)

7.0

Incident Tracking and Corrective Action

Structured reference section aligned to the Natural Alternatives compliance presentation standard.

#

Incident tracking is not a reactive obligation — it is a continuous improvement mechanism. Every incident, near-miss, work refusal, and complaint recorded in the Tier 3 system feeds directly into the corrective action register, which tracks identified deficiencies through to resolution.

Incident Record Requirements

  • Date, time, and location of the incident or near-miss
  • Names of involved workers, witnesses, and the investigating supervisor
  • Description of what occurred — factual, not interpretive
  • Immediate corrective actions taken at the scene
  • Root cause determination following investigation
  • Systemic corrective action assigned, with responsible party and target date
  • Document revision triggered, if applicable — linked to Tier 2 regeneration
  • WSIB reporting status and Critical Injury notification, where applicable

An incident that results in a corrective action, a document revision, and a training update — all recorded and traceable — is evidence of a functioning compliance system. An incident with no follow-up record is evidence of a compliance failure.

Section 9.0

Access, Authorization, and Activation Record

Tier 3 is activated when the employer has completed Tier 1 orientation, generated and approved an initial document suite through Tier 2, and populated the Tier 3 operational infrastructure with those approved documents and their supporting records.

  • Tier 1 and Tier 2 have been completed before Tier 3 operations are initiated
  • Approved documents from Tier 2 are filed in the controlled Tier 3 file structure upon approval
  • Training records are created and filed for every document communicated to workers
  • The Document Control Register reflects all currently active, superseded, and archived documents
  • The Compliance Calendar is populated with all recurring obligations and review dates
  • The Corrective Action Register is maintained and reviewed on a regular schedule
  • The employer retains all records — Natural Alternatives does not store records on the employer's behalf
  • The system is reviewed, maintained, and updated as a living operational framework — not treated as static paperwork
