Purpose, Scope, and Generation Architecture

Structured reference section aligned to the Natural Alternatives compliance presentation standard.

#

The OCOS Tier 2 AI-Driven Compliance Generation Framework is the production engine of the Ontario Compliance Operating System™. It enables Ontario employers to generate enterprise-quality, legislatively-referenced compliance documentation from their specific operational details — without starting from a blank page, relying on generic templates, or engaging costly consultants for every document update.

Tier 2 is not a document library. It is a structured generation system built on a proprietary AI prompt architecture. The system produces tailored draft outputs that reflect the employer's actual workplace — its sector, equipment, crew structure, hazard profile, and supervisory model — rather than a generic facsimile of compliance documentation.

What Tier 2 Produces

  • Written occupational health and safety policies aligned to the OHSA
  • Workplace violence and harassment policies and programs
  • Supervisor responsibility standards and competency frameworks
  • Safe work procedures for sector-specific tasks and equipment
  • Equipment inspection checklists and pre-operation forms
  • Incident investigation and reporting documentation
  • Worker orientation and training record templates
  • JHSC terms of reference and meeting record frameworks
  • Corrective action logs and hazard identification forms
  • Return-to-work and modified duty documentation

What Tier 2 Does Not Do

  • Verify that the employer's operational details are accurate or complete
  • Confirm that a generated document reflects actual workplace conditions
  • Approve, implement, train on, or enforce any document
  • Store, retain, or manage compliance records on the employer's behalf
  • Provide legal advice, regulatory interpretation, or site-specific compliance consulting
  • Guarantee compliance with any statute, regulation, or standard

AI generates the document. The employer reviews, verifies, approves, implements, trains on, and retains the document. This human-in-the-loop process is mandatory — not optional.

The RCCF Prompt Framework

Structured reference section aligned to the Natural Alternatives compliance presentation standard.

#

Every generator in the OCOS Compliance Generator Library is built on the proprietary Role–Context–Command–Format (RCCF) framework. This architecture controls the AI generation interaction at the input level to ensure that outputs are consistent, legislatively-aligned, and structured for Ontario compliance documentation — not the product of casual AI conversation.

R
Role

Defines who the AI is acting as. Sets the expertise, jurisdiction, and perspective used to draft the document. Example: an Ontario occupational health and safety compliance specialist with sector-specific experience.

C
Context

Explains the employer's business situation and the purpose of the document. Inputs from the Company Master Prompt feed this component — ensuring output reflects the actual workplace, not a generic operation.

C
Command

Tells the AI precisely what to produce. Commands are clear, single-task instructions. One generator. One document. One output per session. Compound commands reduce output quality.

F
Format

Defines the required document structure, section order, OCOS document control elements, and formatting standards. Ensures every output integrates into the broader OCOS document architecture.

How to Use the RCCF Framework

1
Open your AI session

Access the OCOS Tier 2 Notion workspace and open a new AI session. Do not carry unrelated conversation history into a compliance generation session.

2
Paste the Company Master Prompt

Paste your completed Company Master Prompt at the beginning of the session. This establishes the employer's operational context, sector, equipment, workforce size, geographic area, and hazard profile before any generation begins.

3
Select and paste one generator prompt

From the Compliance Generator Library, select the appropriate generator for the document you need. Paste the complete RCCF generator prompt below the Company Master Prompt. Do not modify the generator prompt before the AI responds.

4
Review the generated output

Review the draft output against actual workplace conditions, hazards, equipment, and crew structure. The output is a draft — not an approved document.

5
Approve and implement through Tier 3

Reviewed and verified documents are approved through the employer's authorization process, issued to the workplace, and managed through the Tier 3 Compliance Operations System.

The RCCF framework is what separates the OCOS generation system from generic AI use. It controls the input so that the output is consistent, professional, and aligned with Ontario compliance requirements.

Output Standards and Quality Control

Structured reference section aligned to the Natural Alternatives compliance presentation standard.

#

All outputs generated through the OCOS Tier 2 system are subject to consistent quality standards. These standards ensure that generated documents integrate cleanly into the OCOS document architecture and function as defensible workplace controls once approved.

Every Generated Output Must

  • Address a specific workplace hazard, legal requirement, or operational need identified by the employer
  • Reflect the actual conditions, equipment, and processes of the workplace — not generic assumptions
  • Reference the applicable Ontario statute or regulation by correct citation
  • Use terminology consistent with the OCOS framework and the OHSA
  • Carry document control elements: document code, version, status, prepared by, review date
  • Treat the employer's review and approval as a mandatory precondition to implementation
  • Be regenerated — not just edited — when operational conditions change substantially

Employer Review and Approval Requirements

Structured reference section aligned to the Natural Alternatives compliance presentation standard.

#

No document generated through the OCOS Tier 2 system becomes an operational workplace control until it has been formally reviewed, verified, and approved by the employer. This is the most critical step in the OCOS workflow — and the one most commonly bypassed by employers who misunderstand the system's purpose.

A well-formatted AI-generated document that has not been reviewed, approved, communicated, and enforced provides no legal protection to the employer and no safety benefit to the worker. The format is not the control. The implementation is the control.

OCOS Employer Responsibility Principle

Employer Review Checklist

  • Does the document accurately describe the hazards present in our actual workplace?
  • Are the control measures described realistic and enforceable with our equipment and crew?
  • Are the roles and responsibilities assigned to positions that exist in our organization?
  • Are the legislative references current and applicable to our sector and operations?
  • Does the document reflect our actual supervisory structure — not a generic hierarchy?
  • Can we train workers on this document using the content as written?
  • Does this document connect correctly to other documents in our compliance system?
  • Have we documented our review so that it forms part of the due diligence record?
Section 9.0

Access, Authorization, and Handoff to Tier 3

The Tier 2 generation framework is accessed through the OCOS Notion workspace. Authorization to use the generation system is granted at the employer level. All generated and approved documents are organized and managed through the Tier 3 Compliance Operations System.

  • Tier 1 orientation has been completed before Tier 2 generation tools are used
  • The Company Master Prompt accurately reflects current operations before each generation session
  • Every generated output is treated as a draft until reviewed and formally approved by the authorized signatory
  • No generated document is issued to workers, filed as a compliance record, or used in an MOL response until fully approved
  • Approved documents are transferred to the Tier 3 Compliance Operations System for ongoing management
  • The employer retains full responsibility for the accuracy, implementation, and maintenance of all documents
