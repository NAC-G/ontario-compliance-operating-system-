Guideline 01

Purpose of These Guidelines

These guidelines provide operational direction for OCOS Partner License holders deploying the Ontario Compliance Operating System™ across client organizations. They sit alongside — and do not replace — the OCOS Partner License Terms of Use (OCOS-PRAC-TOU-001).

The OCOS Partner License is designed for compliance professionals who add value to the OCOS system through their expertise — sector knowledge, employer-specific customization, implementation management, and ongoing compliance support. These guidelines describe how to deploy the system in a way that serves clients well, protects the integrity of the OCOS framework, and maintains compliance with the license terms.

Read the Terms of Use First

These guidelines are operational in nature. The OCOS Partner License Terms of Use govern the legal relationship between the OCOS Partner and Natural Alternatives. If there is any conflict between these guidelines and the Terms of Use, the Terms of Use govern.

Guideline 02

Before You Deploy — Eligibility and Setup

OCOS Partner Eligibility

The OCOS Partner License is appropriate for individuals and firms who meet one or more of the following profiles:

  • HR consultants delivering compliance services to Ontario employer clients
  • Occupational health and safety consultants managing workplace safety programs for multiple employers
  • Safety officers deploying compliance infrastructure across multiple locations or entities within a common employer group

If you are uncertain whether your practice qualifies, contact Natural Alternatives before purchasing. Licenses are issued on application and Natural Alternatives reserves the right to decline applications that do not meet eligibility criteria.

Setup Steps Before First Deployment

  1. Confirm receipt of your OCOS Partner License confirmation and white-label workspace access from Natural Alternatives.
  2. Read the OCOS Partner License Terms of Use (OCOS-PRAC-TOU-001) in full and sign the acknowledgement.
  3. Complete orientation on all three OCOS tiers before deploying for clients — you should be fully familiar with the Foundation Guide, Generation Framework, and Operations System before guiding a client through any of them.
  4. Set up your white-label Notion workspaces if deploying under your own branding — follow the white-label setup instructions provided with your license materials.
  5. Prepare your client intake process — before beginning any OCOS deployment, you should have the information needed to complete the Company Master Prompt accurately for that client.
Guideline 03

Standard Deployment Workflow

The recommended OCOS deployment sequence for client engagements is as follows:

1
Client Intake
Collect Operational Information

Before opening any generator, gather the client's sector, employee count, supervisory structure, equipment list, applicable regulations, union status, and any existing compliance documents. This information forms the foundation of the Company Master Prompt and directly determines the quality of every generated document.

2
Tier 1 — Orientation
Complete Client Orientation

Walk the employer through the Foundation Guide. Ensure the employer understands their obligations under the OHSA, their role in the IRS, and how the OCOS system is structured before any documents are generated. Obtain employer sign-off on the Tier 1 authorization record. Do not skip this step — it establishes the legal knowledge foundation.

3
Tier 2 — Generation
Build the Company Master Prompt and Generate Documents

Build the client's Company Master Prompt using their operational information. Select generators from the Generator Library appropriate to the client's sector and risk profile. Run each generator, review the output against the client's actual workplace, and edit before presenting to the employer. Do not deliver AI output to a client without professional review.

4
Tier 2 — Review
Employer Review and Approval

Present reviewed drafts to the employer. Walk through each document and confirm it accurately reflects their workplace. Obtain employer approval and signature before any document is filed or communicated to workers. Remind the employer that they are responsible for the accuracy of these documents — your review does not replace theirs.

5
Tier 3 — Operations
Activate the Operations System

File approved documents into the controlled structure. Set up the training register, inspection log, and corrective action register. Configure the compliance calendar. Brief the employer on their ongoing obligations to maintain the system — OCOS deployment is not a one-time event, it is the beginning of an ongoing operating discipline.

6
Ongoing
Maintenance and Update Support

Advise clients of legislative updates that affect their compliance program. Use legislative update notifications from Natural Alternatives to trigger document reviews. Encourage clients to maintain their inspection logs, training records, and corrective action registers on an ongoing basis — these are what convert a compliance program from paper to evidence.

Guideline 04

Client Communication Standards

How you communicate the OCOS system to clients matters — both for their outcomes and for the integrity of the program. The following standards apply to all client-facing communications about OCOS deployments.

Required Disclosures

You must communicate the following to every client before or at the start of an OCOS deployment:

  • The OCOS system produces draft compliance documents. All drafts require employer review, customization, and approval before implementation.
  • The OCOS system is not legal advice. Deployment does not guarantee regulatory compliance or satisfy any legal obligation on its own.
  • The employer retains full responsibility for the accuracy and implementation of all compliance documents and programs.
  • If the employer has specific legal questions about their obligations under Ontario legislation, they should consult a lawyer or licensed professional.

Prohibited Representations

  • Do not represent OCOS-generated documents as having been prepared by a lawyer unless you are a lawyer and have independently reviewed them in that capacity.
  • Do not represent that OCOS deployment certifies, guarantees, or satisfies any specific regulatory requirement.
  • Do not represent that an employer is "compliant" or "inspection-ready" solely on the basis of OCOS document generation — compliance is an operational state that requires implementation, not just documentation.
  • Do not claim that OCOS is your own independently developed framework or intellectual property.

Appropriate Representations

  • You may describe yourself as an OCOS-licensed compliance practitioner.
  • You may describe the OCOS system as a structured, Ontario-aligned compliance framework.
  • You may accurately represent the system's alignment with the OHSA and applicable Ontario regulations.
  • You may represent that OCOS-generated documents, when properly reviewed and implemented, are designed to support a defensible due diligence record.
Guideline 05

White-Label Deployment Protocol

White-label deployment means presenting the OCOS system to clients under your own practice branding rather than Natural Alternatives' branding. The following protocol governs white-label deployments.

Setting Up White-Label Workspaces

  1. Use only the white-label Notion workspace templates provided by Natural Alternatives as part of your OCOS Partner License materials. Do not attempt to create white-label templates independently from the standard workspaces.
  2. Replace Natural Alternatives' name and logo with your own firm name and branding in the designated areas of the white-label templates.
  3. Update the contact information in the workspace to reflect your practice.
  4. Do not alter the structural framework, document control sections, or OCOS architecture components — these form the integrity of the system.

What Clients See in White-Label Deployments

In a white-label deployment, clients see your branding, not Natural Alternatives'. This is intentional. Clients do not need to know the name of the underlying licensed framework — they need to know that their compliance program is professionally built on a structured, Ontario-aligned system, delivered by you.

If a client directly asks what system you are using, you may respond that it is built on a licensed compliance framework developed for Ontario employers, without being required to name the specific product.

White-Label Boundaries

  • You may not remove the structural architecture of the OCOS system from white-label deployments.
  • You may not represent the white-labeled system as your own original intellectual property.
  • You may not share white-label workspace access with other practitioners, firms, or individuals outside your own organization.
Guideline 06

Document Review and Quality Standards

Every document generated through the OCOS Generator Library requires practitioner review before delivery to a client. This is not optional — it is the standard of care for professional OCOS deployment.

Minimum Review Standards

  • Confirm that the document accurately reflects the client's specific workplace — sector, equipment, crew structure, and applicable hazards.
  • Confirm that all regulatory references in the document are current and applicable to the client's operations.
  • Confirm that names, roles, and organizational details in the document match the client's actual structure.
  • Remove or replace any placeholder language that was not updated during generation.
  • Confirm that the document is consistent with other documents generated for the same client — there should be no contradictions between a client's policy documents and their safe work procedures.

Employer Review is Not Optional

OCOS Partner review does not replace employer review. Every document must be reviewed and approved by the employer before it is filed or communicated to workers. Your role is to ensure the document is professionally sound before the employer reviews it — not to substitute for that review.

Quality Standard

A document that is generated and delivered without review does not serve the client — it creates the appearance of compliance without the substance. The OCOS system is designed to produce genuine compliance infrastructure, and that requires professional judgment at the review stage.

Guideline 08

Pre-Deployment Client Checklist

Complete this checklist before beginning an OCOS deployment for each new client organization.

  • Client intake completed — sector, employee count, supervisory structure, equipment, applicable regulations, and existing documentation collected.
  • Client briefed on system scope — employer understands OCOS produces draft documents requiring their review and approval.
  • Disclaimer communicated — employer understands this is not legal advice and does not guarantee regulatory compliance.
  • Employer responsibilities confirmed — employer acknowledges their responsibility for document accuracy and implementation.
  • Tier 1 orientation scheduled or completed — employer has read or will be walked through the Foundation Guide before generation begins.
  • Company Master Prompt drafted — client-specific operational information entered and reviewed for accuracy.
  • Generator selection confirmed — documents to be generated identified and appropriate to the client's sector and risk profile.
  • White-label workspace prepared (if applicable) — client-facing workspace branded, structured, and ready for deployment.
  • Document review workflow confirmed — practitioner review and employer approval steps are scheduled before any documents are filed or communicated.
  • Ongoing maintenance plan discussed — employer understands their ongoing obligations to maintain inspection logs, training records, and corrective action registers.
