What the OCOS Partner License Tiers Include
01 · Client Deployments
Client Organization Deployments

Deploy the full OCOS system — all three tiers — across client organizations based on your tier (Starter: 3 clients, Growth: 7 clients, Pro: 15 clients, Agency: 30 clients). Each client receives a customized, employer-specific compliance program built on the OCOS architecture and tailored to their specific operation, sector, and crew structure.

02 · White-Label Access
White-Label Notion Workspace Templates

Access to white-label versions of all three Notion workspaces. Remove Natural Alternatives branding and deploy under your own practice name. Customizable workspace structure, generator labels, and client-facing documentation — your brand, the OCOS infrastructure. Subject to white-label deployment guidelines.

03 · Legislative Updates
Ontario Legislative Update Notifications

Direct notifications when Ontario occupational health and safety legislation changes in a way that affects the OCOS system. Each notification includes a plain-language summary of the change, which generators or documents are affected, and recommended client action. Stay ahead of amendments so your clients stay compliant.

04 · Generator Releases
New Generator Library Releases

All new compliance generator types released during your license year are included automatically. As the OCOS Generator Library expands — new sectors, new document types, new regulatory frameworks — OCOS Partner License holders receive access without additional cost for the current license term.

OCOS Partner License — Who It's For
HR Consultants Firms and independent consultants building compliance programs for Ontario employer clients as part of a broader HR service mandate.
OHS Consultants Occupational health and safety professionals managing ongoing compliance programs for multiple employer clients across Ontario.
Multi-Site Safety Officers In-house safety professionals deploying a standardized compliance infrastructure across multiple locations, divisions, or entities within a common employer group.
