FAQ

Is this legal advice or a compliance guarantee?

No. This product provides draft documentation and an operating structure only. The employer remains responsible for reviewing accuracy, approving content, delivering training, implementing controls, and maintaining the system over time. Different operations require different controls, and no template or prompt pack can replace competent internal review.

Is this software that automatically generates documents?

No. The system is “prompt-driven.” You select a generator, paste the prompt into an AI tool, answer questions about your operation, and then you review and approve the draft output. The system is designed so you can repeatedly generate updated drafts when equipment, tasks, hazards, or crews change.

What makes this different from a typical template pack?

Template packs typically give you static files that start drifting away from real work immediately. This system is structured as an operating model: it tells you what to create, how to keep it controlled, how to track proof-of-implementation, and how to update it without starting from zero. The emphasis is on keeping documents alive and consistent rather than creating documents once and forgetting them.

How do upgrades work for existing customers?

If you previously purchased a lower tier, you can use the Upgrade Purchase section to pay the difference for the higher tier. This keeps checkout simple for returning customers. New customers should purchase normally from the Pricing section to avoid mismatches.

What is “verification and tracking (B6)” in practical terms?

Verification means you can show proof that training happened, inspections were completed, corrective actions were assigned, and issues were actually closed. B6 tools support logs, sign-offs, audit summaries, and simple evidence trails. In practice, this reduces “we did it but can’t prove it” failures during inspections.

Can I edit the documents and brand them for my business?

Yes. The intended workflow is that you generate drafts, copy them into your own internal documentation system, and then edit them so they match your equipment, hazards, and procedures. You are expected to brand, approve, and control your versions internally so workers are using the current version in the field.

Who is Tier 3 designed for?

Tier 3 is designed for organizations that need consistency across multiple crews, multiple sites, or a more formal governance model. In practice, this means you need documentation that stays aligned across supervisors and locations, and you want a stronger audit and corrective-action oversight structure to reduce variation and reduce operational drift.
