1.0 — Welcome to OCOS

Welcome to the Ontario Compliance Operating System™. This package delivers the foundational compliance architecture for your Ontario workplace. Read this guide in full before accessing any other OCOS component.

OCOS provides a structured, traceable, inspection-ready compliance system built on the legislative framework of the Occupational Health and Safety Act, R.S.O. 1990, c. O.1. The system is organized into three progressive tiers, each building on the level before it.

Key Principle: Orientation before generation. Generation before implementation. Implementation before reliance. Do not skip a stage.

4.0 — How to Use Tier 1

Follow these steps in order before proceeding to Tier 2 or Tier 3:

  • Read the Foundation Guide (OCOS-T1-FDN-001) in full
  • Review your legal duties under the OHSA (Sections 4–5 of the Foundation Guide)
  • Complete the Employer Implementation Checklist (OCOS-T1-CHK-001)
  • Follow the Implementation Guide (OCOS-T1-IMP-001) to deploy the system
  • Complete the Document Control Template with your organization's details
  • Proceed to Tier 2 to generate your operational compliance documents

1.0 — Purpose and Scope

This guide explains how organizations transition from reviewing the OCOS Tier 1 Foundation Guide to actively implementing the compliance system within their workplace. It covers organizational preparation, document development, training and communication, record retention setup, and system maintenance.

This guide should be used alongside the Employer Implementation Checklist (OCOS-T1-CHK-001). The Checklist confirms completion of each stage; this Guide explains how to execute each stage correctly.

OCOS is a system, not a document set. Its value comes from implementation and active management — not from document existence alone.

2.0 — Stage 1: Organizational Preparation

2.1 Management Review Protocol

The employer or a senior management designate must review the Foundation Guide (OCOS-T1-FDN-001) in full before authorizing any other OCOS activity. The review should cover:

  • The legal duties of employers, supervisors, and workers under the OHSA
  • The OCOS tier architecture and the role of each tier in the compliance program
  • The employer's role in the AI-assisted generation process — specifically the human-in-the-loop review, approval, and implementation obligations
  • The distinction between a draft document and an implemented control

Management review must be documented. The Employer Authorization section of the Foundation Guide (Section 9.0) should be signed upon completion.

2.2 Designating Document Control Authority

Every organization implementing OCOS must designate a specific person or role as the Document Control Authority. This person is responsible for:

  • Maintaining the master document register for all OCOS materials
  • Controlling version numbering and issuing updated documents
  • Ensuring superseded documents are archived and removed from active use
  • Coordinating document review cycles in accordance with Section 7.0 of the Foundation Guide

2.3 Supervisor Orientation Protocol

Before operational compliance documents are introduced to the field, supervisors must be oriented on:

  • Why the OCOS system exists and its legal basis under the OHSA
  • Their specific duties under OHSA s. 27, including the obligation to take every reasonable precaution
  • How to use OCOS documents as active controls, not passive paperwork
  • The enforcement expectation: supervisors must communicate, apply, and document compliance

3.0 — Stage 2: Document Development

3.1 Operational Verification Process

Before any generated document is approved for use, the employer must verify the following against actual workplace conditions:

  • Hazard descriptions accurately reflect the hazards present in this workplace
  • Control measures listed are the controls actually in place or being implemented
  • Equipment and materials referenced match what is used on-site
  • Responsibilities assigned to roles that exist within this organization
  • Training requirements are achievable and aligned with the organization's training program

4.0 — Stage 3: Training and Communication

4.1 Orientation Session Protocol

New workers must receive orientation on all applicable OCOS documents before commencing work. Orientation must cover:

  • The employer's Health and Safety Policy and their rights and responsibilities
  • All Safe Work Procedures applicable to the worker's assigned tasks
  • Hazard identification and reporting procedures
  • Emergency procedures, first aid station locations, and the identity of the first aider
  • PPE requirements and how to obtain, use, and report defects in protective equipment

4.3 Retraining Triggers

  • A document is revised and the revision affects worker responsibilities or procedures
  • An incident, near-miss, or work refusal reveals a gap in understanding or compliance
  • A worker is assigned new tasks or equipment not covered in their original orientation
  • An MOL inspection or internal audit identifies a training deficiency

6.0 — Stage 5: System Maintenance

6.1 OCOS System Health Indicators

  • Inspection records are being completed on schedule and filed
  • Training records are current for all workers across all applicable procedures
  • Corrective actions from incidents and inspections are tracked to close-out
  • Documents have been reviewed within their scheduled review cycle
  • Supervisors are using OCOS documents as active controls, not referencing them only after incidents

1.0 — Implementation Orientation

This checklist guides employers through the steps required to implement the Ontario Compliance Operating System™ Tier 1 Compliance Foundation within their workplace. Complete each item in sequence before proceeding to Tier 2 generation.

All items must be completed and confirmed before the OCOS system is considered activated for Tier 2 or Tier 3 use.

2.0 — Management Review

  • Foundation Guide (OCOS-T1-FDN-001) reviewed by employer or senior management in full
  • Legal responsibilities under the Occupational Health and Safety Act, R.S.O. 1990, c. O.1 reviewed and understood
  • OCOS tier architecture reviewed — Tier 1 (Foundation), Tier 2 (AI-Driven Generation), Tier 3 (Compliance Operations)
  • Organizational compliance objectives identified
  • Document control expectations and version control procedures understood
  • AI-assisted generation model and employer review responsibilities understood
  • RCCF prompt framework (Role, Context, Command, Format) understood

3.0 — Document Control Authority

  • Person responsible for document control identified and designated
  • Document approval pathway established (who reviews, who approves, who releases)
  • Document storage location confirmed (Notion workspace, shared drive, or file server)
  • Version control method understood — increment vs. regeneration distinction understood
  • Superseded document removal process established

4.0 — Supervisor Orientation

  • Supervisors introduced to the OCOS documentation structure and its legal purpose
  • Supervisors understand their duties under OHSA s. 27
  • Supervisors understand hazard communication responsibilities
  • Supervisors understand enforcement expectations and documentation obligations
  • Supervisors understand the difference between a draft document and an implemented control
  • Supervisor designation confirmed and documented

5.0 — Operational Fit Review

  • Workplace hazards reviewed and identified for this organization
  • Operational procedures and high-risk tasks confirmed
  • Equipment inventory reviewed against OCOS document scope
  • Worker training needs identified and mapped to OHSA requirements
  • Applicable regulations identified (e.g. O. Reg. 213/91 — Construction, O. Reg. 851 — Industrial)
  • JHSC or Health and Safety Representative threshold confirmed (20+ workers = JHSC; 6–19 = Rep required)

6.0 — Legislative Reference Acknowledgment

  • Occupational Health and Safety Act — ontario.ca/laws/statute/90o01
  • O. Reg. 297/13 — OHS Awareness and Training — ontario.ca/laws/regulation/130297
  • O. Reg. 213/91 — Construction Projects — ontario.ca/laws/regulation/910213
  • Workplace Safety and Insurance Act — ontario.ca/laws/statute/97w16
  • WHMIS — canada.ca/en/health-canada

7.0 — Implementation Confirmation

  • Organization understands the purpose of the Tier 1 Foundation Guide
  • Management approval received to proceed with Tier 2 compliance document generation
  • Supervisor orientation completed and documented
  • Document control authority designated
  • Operational fit review completed
  • All legislative references confirmed against current published legislation
